Times Square is famous for its impressive display of electronic billboards and digital advertisements, which illuminate the area with an array of colors and lights. These screens feature advertisements from major brands, showcasing the latest products and entertainment offerings. The sheer magnitude of lights and advertisements contributes to the electric energy and excitement that permeate the square.


Times Square is particularly renowned for its New Year's Eve celebrations. Each year, thousands of people gather in the square to witness the famous ball drop at midnight, marking the beginning of the new year. The event is televised worldwide and has become an iconic tradition. Beyond its commercial and entertainment aspects, Times Square holds historical significance as well. It has witnessed important events throughout history, such as ticker tape parades celebrating achievements and victories.


Whether day or night, Times Square never fails to captivate visitors with its energy, excitement, and vibrant atmosphere. It remains a symbolic and dynamic landmark that encapsulates the spirit of New York City.

Places to Visit

The Glass Building


The Glass Building, also known as the One World Trade Center, is an iconic skyscraper located in Lower Manhattan. Here are some tips and reasons why you should visit it:
Panoramic Views: The Glass Building offers breathtaking panoramic views of the New York City skyline. From the observation deck on the 100th to 102nd floors, you can enjoy stunning vistas of Manhattan, the Hudson River, and beyond.


Symbol of Resilience: The Glass Building stands as a symbol of resilience and strength. It is the main building of the World Trade Center complex, which was rebuilt after the tragic events of September 11, 2001. Visiting this building allows you to pay tribute to the past while embracing the future.
Educational Experience: The Glass Building offers an interactive and educational experience. The elevator ride to the top features a time-lapse video showcasing the development of New York City over the years. The Sky Portal, a 14-foot-wide circular disc with real-time footage of the streets below, provides a unique perspective.

Dining and Shopping: The building houses a variety of dining options and shops. You can indulge in a delicious meal with a view at the One Dine restaurant or explore the retail options at the Westfield World Trade Center mall.

The Christyler Building


The Christyler Building, officially known as the Chrysler Building, is an Art Deco masterpiece located in Midtown Manhattan. Here are some reasons why you should visit:
Architectural Marvel: The Christyler Building is renowned for its distinctive Art Deco design. Its gleaming stainless steel spire and ornate details make it one of the most recognizable and admired buildings in New York City. Architecture enthusiasts will appreciate the craftsmanship and attention to detail.

Observation Deck: While the Christyler Building does not have a public observation deck, it is still worth visiting for its stunning lobby. The lobby showcases beautiful Art Deco elements, including murals, intricate mosaics, and a lavish ceiling. Take a moment to admire the grandeur and elegance of this architectural gem.

Historical Significance: The Christyler Building played a significant role in the history of skyscrapers. When it was completed in 1930, it briefly held the title of the tallest building in the world. It represents the ambitious spirit of the Art Deco era and stands as a testament to human ingenuity and engineering prowess.
Iconic Landmark: The Christyler Building is an iconic symbol of New York City. It has been featured in numerous films, photographs, and artwork, and it continues to captivate people with its timeless beauty. Visiting the building allows you to experience firsthand the allure and magnificence of this architectural icon.

The Statue of Liberty

Symbol of Freedom: The Statue of Liberty is a powerful symbol of freedom and democracy. It was a gift from France to the United States and has become an iconic representation of the American ideals of liberty, democracy, and hope. It has welcomed millions of immigrants to America, making it a symbol of opportunity and a beacon of hope.
Historical Significance: The Statue of Liberty was dedicated on October 28, 1886. It was designed by French sculptor Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi and its metal framework was created by Gustave Eiffel, the designer of the Eiffel Tower. The statue was a joint effort between France and the United States to commemorate the friendship established during the American Revolution.
Design and Features: The statue stands at a height of 305 feet, including the pedestal, and weighs about 450,000 pounds. It is made of copper sheets, which have turned green over time due to oxidation. The statue depicts a female figure representing Libertas, the Roman goddess of freedom, holding a torch in her right hand and a tablet inscribed with the date of the American Declaration of Independence in her left hand.
Access and Visiting: Visitors can access the Statue of Liberty via a ferry service that operates from Battery Park in Manhattan or Liberty State Park in New Jersey. The ferry takes visitors to Liberty Island, where they can explore the grounds, visit the museum, and even climb to the crown or pedestal for panoramic views of the city skyline and the harbor. However, it's essential to check the official website for updated information on access, tickets, and any specific requirements or restrictions.
Cultural and Historical Experience: A visit to the Statue of Liberty offers a unique cultural and historical experience. The museum located on Liberty Island provides insight into the statue's construction, history, and significance. It also highlights the stories of the millions of immigrants who came to America seeking a better life. Exploring the grounds and enjoying the views of the statue and the surrounding area is a memorable experience that allows visitors to connect with America's history and ideals
